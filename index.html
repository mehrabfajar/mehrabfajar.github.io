How I Think

My Approach

From the first conversation to a working system - four principles that shape every project I take on.

01

Understand before you build

Every system starts with clarity about the real outcome it needs to drive - stakeholder context, operational constraints, and clear success criteria come before any code.

02

Prototype fast, fail cheap

A working baseline always beats a stalled ambitious design. I establish a simple, measurable solution early - a foundation to iterate from, not a target to eventually reach.

03

Measure what actually matters

Evaluation is a first-class deliverable. I build frameworks that reflect real-world performance - not just holdout accuracy. If you can't measure the right thing, you can't improve it.

04

Deliver with transparency

Technology creates value only when people trust it. I design systems where decisions are traceable and every stakeholder - technical or not - can engage with confidence.
