Approach

Building Intelligent Systems

Four principles that shape how I approach every project — from early-stage exploration to production-ready deployment.

01

Understand the problem before the data

Every meaningful system starts with clarity about the real-world outcome it needs to drive. I invest time upfront in stakeholder context, operational constraints, and success criteria — so that technical choices serve actual goals, not just benchmarks.

02

Move fast with simple, working foundations

A functional baseline outperforms a stalled ambitious system every time. I establish simple, measurable solutions early — creating a foundation to iterate from rather than a target to eventually reach. Complexity is earned, not assumed.

03

Measure what actually matters

Rigorous evaluation is as important as the solution itself. I build evaluation frameworks that capture real-world performance — not just accuracy on a holdout set. If you can't measure the right thing, you can't improve the right thing.

04

Make decisions transparent and actionable

Technology creates value only when people understand and trust it. I design systems where outputs are explainable, decisions are traceable, and every stakeholder — technical or not — can engage with confidence. Transparency is not a feature; it is the foundation of impact.
